Anti-spoofing: Semantic Manipulation Detection


AV-SEM: Audio-Visual Semantic Consistency Modeling on Semantic Manipulation

Tianjin University

Task Definition. As text-to-speech (TTS), talking head, and other artificial intelligent generated content (AIGC) technologies continue to mature, the threat posed by semantic manipulation of the original target speaker's video has become increasingly severe. This is especially evident in the spread of rumors involving celebrities and the perpetration of telecommunication fraud targeting acquaintances. Given a specific target speaker video, semantically manipulate the original audio with an imposter audio, using speech grafting, TTS, and Wav2Lip techniques. This work primarily focuses on the two most susceptible technologies for fraudulent attacks: speech grafting and lip synchronization adjustments, modeling the temporal consistency between visual and auditory semantics. Experimental tests are conducted in both in-domain (ID) target speakers and unseen speakers. Additionally, this paper presents a comparison of the performance between 10 human evaluators and the proposed machine learning model. Audio samples are available at https://av-sem.github.io/.

Ethics Statement

Since LRS3 database maintains speaker identity, it may carry potential risks in misuse of the compiled data, such as speech synthesis or voice conversion. We conducted the experiments under the assumption that the user agree to use this data under only research purpose. If the data is used in the real world, it should include a protocol to ensure that the speaker approves the use of their voice and videos.
